Module 3: Investigation Best Practices

Timeline Planning

Setting realistic milestones to keep investigations on track

Target Timelines by Classification Level

Investigations must be completed in a timely manner to protect the rights of all parties, preserve evidence, and maintain organizational trust. The target timeline for an investigation depends on its classification level, which reflects the complexity and severity of the matter.

Level 1 — Significant Investigations

Target completion: 30–60 days. Level 1 investigations involve the most serious allegations and typically require extensive evidence collection, multiple interviews, and coordination across functions or with external parties. The longer timeline accounts for this complexity, but the investigation should still be driven toward the earliest feasible completion.

Level 2 — Standard Investigations

Target completion: 15–30 days. Level 2 investigations address matters that, while important, are typically narrower in scope and involve fewer parties. The shorter timeline reflects the expectation that these investigations can proceed more efficiently while still meeting all quality and procedural standards.

Milestone Breakdown

To ensure investigations progress steadily and do not stall, each investigation should be planned around key milestones. The table below provides a general framework for distributing work across the investigation timeline:

Milestone Target Timing Description
Investigation Plan Within 5 days of assignment Complete the scope document, identify evidence sources, prepare an interview list, and establish the investigation timeline. The plan should be reviewed and approved by the Investigation Lead or Coordinator.
Evidence Collection First third of timeline Gather all documentary, physical, digital, and other non-testimonial evidence. Issue preservation notices as needed. Complete chain of custody documentation for all evidence obtained.
Interviews Middle third of timeline Conduct interviews with the complainant, witnesses, and respondent (in that order). Prepare questions in advance, document responses contemporaneously, and allow interviewees to review their statements.
Analysis & Reporting Final third of timeline Analyze all collected evidence, apply the applicable standard of proof, draft the investigation report, and submit findings and conclusions to the Investigation Lead for review.
Investigation Plan: 5 Days The investigation plan must be completed within 5 days of the investigator being assigned. This is a firm milestone that applies to both Level 1 and Level 2 investigations, regardless of the overall target timeline.
⚠
Timeline Extensions: If an investigation cannot be completed within its target timeline, the investigator must document the reason for the extension, obtain approval from the Investigation Lead or Coordinator, and communicate the revised timeline to relevant stakeholders. Common reasons for extensions include difficulty locating witnesses, delays in obtaining records from third parties, or the emergence of new allegations that expand the scope. Undocumented extensions are not acceptable and may compromise the defensibility of the investigation.
ⓘ
Progress Tracking: The Investigation Lead should conduct regular check-ins with the investigator to monitor progress against milestones. For Level 1 investigations, weekly check-ins are recommended. For Level 2, check-ins at the midpoint and prior to the target completion date are generally sufficient.

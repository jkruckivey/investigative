Henderson File: Resolution

Closing the investigation into falsified production logs

Throughout this course, you have followed the Henderson File from its initial anonymous complaint through intake, classification, planning, evidence collection, interviews, and analysis. It is now time to bring this investigation to its conclusion.

The Henderson File — Investigation Conclusion

Evidence Summary

The investigation into Marcus Chen, Plant Supervisor at the Henderson Road Processing Plant, examined the allegation that Chen had been falsifying production quality inspection logs and instructing line workers to skip inspection checkpoints during high-volume production runs. The following evidence was gathered and analyzed:

Production Log Gaps

A comprehensive review of production inspection logs over the three-month period identified by the complainant revealed significant gaps. On 14 separate dates, inspection checkpoints were marked as completed, but the corresponding data fields that would have been populated during an actual inspection were left blank or contained identical entries copied from previous shifts. The gaps correlated with high-volume production periods.

Digital Access Records

System access logs confirmed that Marcus Chen was the user logged into the production management system during the times the flagged inspection entries were created or modified. Badge access records placed Chen on the production floor during these same periods, confirming his physical presence and opportunity.

Corroborating Witness Statements

Two production line workers, interviewed separately, provided consistent accounts that Chen had verbally instructed them to "check the boxes" on inspection forms without performing the actual inspections during periods when production targets were at risk of being missed. Both witnesses described similar language and circumstances, and their accounts were consistent with the timeline established by the production log gaps.

Physical Evidence

A physical inspection of equipment and inspection points on the production line revealed that several checkpoints showed signs of being routinely skipped. Calibration stickers on two pieces of inspection equipment were outdated, and the inspection stations showed no wear patterns consistent with regular use during the relevant period.

Conclusion: Substantiated

Based on the totality of the evidence, and applying the balance of probabilities standard, the allegation that Marcus Chen falsified production quality inspection logs is Substantiated. The production log gaps, digital access records placing Chen at the system during the times entries were created, two independent corroborating witness statements, and physical evidence of skipped inspections collectively establish that the alleged conduct more likely than not occurred. Chen was given a full opportunity to respond to the allegations during his interview but was unable to provide a satisfactory explanation for the evidence.

Communication of Outcomes

The investigation report and its findings were communicated to the following parties:

FSQA Leadership Findings related to food safety process failures and recommended corrective actions for inspection protocols Responsible for food safety compliance and inspection process integrity
Respondent (Marcus Chen) The findings relevant to him, the conclusion (Substantiated), and the corrective actions to be taken Entitled to know the outcome and any actions affecting his employment

Recommended Actions

The following actions were recommended to the decision-maker:

  • Disciplinary action: Formal disciplinary measures for Marcus Chen, up to and including termination, given the seriousness of falsifying food safety records and the potential risk to public health
  • Retraining: Mandatory retraining on food safety inspection procedures and the SOP for all supervisors at the Henderson Road plant
  • Enhanced inspection protocols: Implementation of additional verification steps for production inspection logs, including random audits and dual-sign-off requirements for high-volume production runs
  • Audit of other shifts: A review of inspection logs from other shifts and supervisors at the Henderson Road facility to determine whether similar practices exist beyond Chen's direct oversight

File Closure and Archival

The Henderson File investigation is now closed. The complete investigation file—including the original EthicsLine complaint, the investigation plan, all documentary and digital evidence, interview notes, chain of custody records, the final report, and all communication records—has been archived in accordance with Maple Leaf Foods' records retention policy. The file will be retained for a minimum of 7 years in secure storage with access limited to authorized personnel.

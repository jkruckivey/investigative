Batch 447: Product Contamination

A food safety investigation requiring urgent action

You are an FSQA investigator at Maple Leaf Foods. The following food safety deviation has been identified through routine quality testing. Read the details carefully—this investigation involves public health implications and regulatory obligations that add urgency and complexity.

Scenario

During routine quality testing, the FSQA lab identifies elevated levels of Listeria monocytogenes in Batch 447 of ready-to-eat deli meat produced at the Hamilton facility. The batch was produced over a 3-day run and approximately 15,000 kg of product has already been distributed to retail customers. The production line has been shut down pending investigation.

Regulatory obligations: Listeria monocytogenes contamination in ready-to-eat meat products triggers mandatory reporting requirements under CFIA (Canadian Food Inspection Agency) regulations. A potential product recall must be evaluated immediately. Time is critical in this investigation.

Evidence Summary

Lab Results

Listeria monocytogenes was confirmed at elevated levels in multiple samples from Batch 447. Environmental swabs from the production line also returned positive results, particularly in areas downstream of the pasteurizer and near conveyor belt joints that are addressed during CIP cycles.

Sanitation Records

A scheduled CIP cycle was skipped on Day 2 of the 3-day production run. The skip was authorized by the shift supervisor due to production schedule pressure. No formal deviation report was filed for the skipped CIP, and no compensating controls were implemented. Previous months' records show two other instances where CIP cycles were delayed, though not fully skipped.

Equipment Records

The pasteurizer experienced a temperature fluctuation on Day 1, dropping below the critical control limit for approximately 8 minutes. A deviation report was filed, but the affected product was not held for additional testing. Maintenance records show the temperature sensor had been flagged for calibration two weeks prior but the calibration was not completed.

Interview Findings

Production staff confirmed that schedule pressure frequently led to shortened or skipped sanitation cycles. The shift supervisor stated they believed a shortened CIP was acceptable because "we had never had an issue before." Maintenance personnel confirmed the overdue sensor calibration and stated that parts delays contributed to the backlog.

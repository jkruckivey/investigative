Module 5: Documentation & Closure

Records Retention & Security

Maintaining investigation files securely and in compliance with retention requirements

When an investigation is closed, the investigation file does not disappear. It becomes a permanent organizational record that must be maintained in accordance with Maple Leaf Foods' records retention policies and applicable legal requirements. Proper retention and security of investigation files protects the organization, the parties involved, and the integrity of the investigative process.

Retention Requirements

Minimum 7-Year Retention Period

All investigation files must be retained for a minimum of 7 years from the date of file closure. This minimum applies regardless of the investigation level, the outcome, or the nature of the allegations. Some matters—particularly those involving regulatory obligations, ongoing litigation, or serious safety concerns—may require longer retention periods as determined by Legal Counsel.

Secure Storage

Investigation files must be stored securely, whether in physical or electronic form. Physical files must be kept in locked storage with controlled access. Electronic files must be stored in secure, access-controlled systems with appropriate encryption and audit logging. Files must not be stored on personal devices, shared drives without access restrictions, or any unsecured location.

Access Limited to Authorized Personnel

Access to investigation files is restricted to authorized personnel on a need-to-know basis. Authorized personnel typically include the investigator, the Investigation Lead, Legal Counsel, and designated records management staff. Access requests from others—including senior leaders—must be evaluated against the need-to-know standard and approved by the appropriate authority.

What Is Included in the Investigation File

The investigation file is a complete record of the investigation from intake to closure. It must contain all of the following:

Document Description
Original complaint The initial report or complaint as received, including the intake form and any supporting materials submitted by the complainant
Investigation plan The documented plan outlining the scope, methodology, evidence to be collected, witnesses to be interviewed, and timeline
All evidence collected Copies of all documentary, digital, and physical evidence gathered during the investigation, including chain of custody records
Interview notes Detailed notes or transcripts from all interviews conducted, including dates, participants, and any signed witness statements
Correspondence All communications related to the investigation, including preservation notices, interview scheduling, and communications with Legal or HR
Final report The complete, finalized investigation report including executive summary, scope, methodology, findings, analysis, conclusion, and recommendations
Communication records Documentation of all outcome communications: who was informed, what they were told, when, and how
⚠
Do not destroy files prematurely. Investigation files may be needed long after the investigation is closed—for subsequent complaints, legal proceedings, regulatory audits, or organizational reviews. Never dispose of investigation files before the minimum retention period has elapsed, and always check with Legal before disposing of any file, particularly those involving significant matters.
ⓘ
Litigation holds: If an investigation file is subject to a litigation hold (a legal instruction to preserve records related to anticipated or active litigation), the file must be retained indefinitely until the hold is released by Legal Counsel, regardless of the standard retention period.

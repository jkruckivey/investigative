Warehouse Near-Miss Investigation

Investigating a near-miss to prevent future incidents

You are an OHS investigator at Maple Leaf Foods. The following near-miss incident has been reported through the formal safety reporting process. Near-miss investigations are critical because they reveal hazards before they result in actual injuries. Read the details carefully.

Scenario

A forklift operator at the Mississauga distribution warehouse narrowly avoided striking a pedestrian worker who was walking in a designated forklift aisle without a high-visibility vest. The forklift operator swerved and collided with racking, causing minor damage. No injuries occurred but the incident was captured on warehouse CCTV. Both the forklift operator (James Rivera) and the pedestrian worker (Anika Patel) are shaken. A supervisor filed the near-miss report.

⚠
Near-miss significance: Near-miss incidents are leading indicators of potential serious injuries or fatalities. Research consistently shows that for every serious workplace injury, there are hundreds of near-misses. Investigating near-misses thoroughly is one of the most effective ways to prevent future incidents.

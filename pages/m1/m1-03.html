Module 1: The Investigative Framework

Guiding Principles

The foundational standards that govern every investigation

The Enterprise-Wide Investigative Procedure is built on five guiding principles. These principles are not aspirational—they are mandatory standards that apply to every investigation, regardless of function, complexity, or level. They serve as the ethical and procedural foundation for all investigative work at Maple Leaf Foods.

Fairness & Impartiality

Every investigation must be conducted without bias, prejudice, or predetermined conclusions. All parties—including the complainant, respondent, and witnesses—must be treated with respect and given a genuine opportunity to be heard.

Investigators must be free from conflicts of interest. If a conflict exists or is perceived, the investigator must disclose it immediately and may need to be replaced. Fairness also means that the respondent is presumed innocent until the evidence establishes otherwise; the investigation exists to find facts, not to confirm assumptions.

Confidentiality

Information related to the investigation must be shared only on a need-to-know basis. All parties involved in an investigation—investigators, witnesses, complainants, and respondents—are expected to maintain confidentiality about the existence and details of the investigation.

Confidentiality protects the integrity of the investigation, prevents the contamination of evidence, and safeguards the privacy and reputation of all individuals involved. However, confidentiality is not absolute: legal obligations, safety concerns, or regulatory requirements may require disclosure.

Thoroughness & Timeliness

Investigations must be conducted with sufficient depth to gather all relevant facts and reach a well-supported conclusion. At the same time, they must be completed in a timely manner. Unreasonable delays can compromise evidence, erode trust, and increase risk to the organization.

Thoroughness means pursuing all reasonable lines of inquiry, interviewing all relevant witnesses, and collecting all available evidence. Timeliness means adhering to the expected timelines for each investigation level and escalating promptly when delays are unavoidable.

Consistency

Similar matters must be handled in a consistent manner across all functions and locations. Consistency does not mean that every investigation is identical—the scope and approach will vary depending on the nature and complexity of the matter. However, the process, documentation standards, and decision-making criteria should be applied uniformly.

Consistency builds trust in the process and reduces the risk of claims of unfair treatment or discrimination. It also supports the organization's ability to identify patterns and systemic issues across functions.

Legal Compliance

All investigations must comply with applicable federal, provincial, and local laws and regulations. This includes, but is not limited to, employment and labour standards, occupational health and safety legislation, food safety regulations, privacy laws, and human rights legislation.

Investigators must be aware of the legal requirements relevant to their function and jurisdiction. When in doubt, Legal Counsel should be consulted. Failure to comply with legal requirements can expose the organization to regulatory penalties, litigation, and reputational harm.

Module 2: Intake, Classification & Escalation

Intake & Acknowledgement

Receiving, documenting, and acknowledging incoming complaints

Designated Intake Channels

All complaints and reports that may require investigation must be received through one of Maple Leaf Foods' designated intake channels. Using established channels ensures that every report is captured, tracked, and routed appropriately from the outset.

EthicsLine

Maple Leaf Foods' confidential, third-party reporting hotline available 24/7. Reports can be made by phone or online. The EthicsLine accepts anonymous submissions and is the primary channel for whistleblower complaints, ethical concerns, and matters the reporter may not feel comfortable raising directly.

Direct Report to Manager

Employees may raise concerns directly with their immediate supervisor or another manager in their reporting chain. Managers who receive such reports are obligated to document the complaint and escalate it to the appropriate function promptly.

Human Resources

HR representatives serve as a primary intake point for workplace conduct concerns including harassment, discrimination, policy violations, and interpersonal conflict. HR intake staff are trained to gather initial details and assess whether a formal investigation is warranted.

⚠
Protecting Complainant Identity: Regardless of the intake channel used, the identity of the complainant must be safeguarded to the greatest extent possible. Share identifying information only with those who have a direct need to know for the purposes of the investigation. Anonymous complaints received through the EthicsLine must remain anonymous unless the reporter voluntarily identifies themselves.

Acknowledgement Timeline

Every complaint received through a designated intake channel must be formally acknowledged within 2 business days of receipt. This acknowledgement serves several important purposes:

  • Confirms that the report has been received and is being taken seriously
  • Sets expectations with the complainant about the process and next steps
  • Provides an opportunity to explain confidentiality protections
  • Creates a documented record of when the organization became aware of the matter
2 Business Days The maximum allowable time between receipt of a complaint and formal acknowledgement to the complainant. This applies to all intake channels. For anonymous EthicsLine reports, acknowledgement is provided through the EthicsLine platform.

Initial Documentation Requirements

At the intake stage, the receiving party must capture and document the following information to the extent it is available:

Element Details
Date & time of report When the complaint was received, including the channel through which it arrived
Complainant identity Name and contact information (or "anonymous" if submitted through EthicsLine without identification)
Subject(s) of complaint The individual(s) or area(s) the complaint is about, if identified
Nature of allegation Brief description of what is alleged to have occurred
Relevant dates & location When and where the alleged events took place
Witnesses identified Any individuals mentioned as having witnessed or having knowledge of the matter
Supporting materials Any documents, photos, or other evidence provided by the complainant at intake
ⓘ
Remember: Not every complaint will arrive with complete information. The intake stage is about capturing what is available and initiating the classification process. Gaps in detail will be addressed during the completeness check in the Classification Framework (covered next).

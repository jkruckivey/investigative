Module 5: Documentation & Closure

Closing a Level 3 Matter

Proper documentation even when no formal investigation is conducted

Not every complaint results in a formal investigation. Level 3 matters are those that, after assessment, do not warrant a formal investigation. However, the fact that no investigation was conducted does not mean no documentation is required. Proper closure documentation protects the organization by creating a record that the complaint was received, assessed, and handled appropriately.

⚠
Even without a formal investigation, documentation is mandatory. A Level 3 closure file demonstrates that the organization did not ignore the complaint—it assessed the matter and made a defensible decision not to investigate further.

Level 3 Closure: Walkthrough

Scenario — Anonymous Tip about Customer Service

An anonymous tip is submitted through the EthicsLine reporting that an employee in the customer service department is "rude to customers." The tip does not identify any specific incidents, dates, customers, or witnesses. The tipster describes the employee by first name only and says the behavior has been happening "for a while."

After assessment, this matter is classified as Level 3. Here is how the closure documentation would be completed:

Complaint Received

Anonymous EthicsLine submission received on November 8. The tipster reported that an employee referred to as "Dana" in the customer service department is "rude to customers." No specific incidents, dates, customers, or witnesses were identified. The tipster stated the behavior has been occurring "for a while."

Assessment Process

The complaint was assessed by J. Park (HR Manager) on November 9. The Classification Framework was applied. The complaint was evaluated against the five severity dimensions. Due to the lack of specific details—no identified incidents, dates, witnesses, or affected customers—the complaint could not be investigated in its current form.

Reason No Investigation Was Conducted

The complaint lacks sufficient specificity to support a formal investigation. No specific conduct, dates, or witnesses are identified. The allegation of being "rude" is too vague to assess against any particular policy or standard without additional context or detail.

Preliminary Steps Taken

J. Park reviewed the customer complaint records for the customer service department for the past 90 days. No formal complaints from customers mentioning rudeness or unprofessional behavior were found. J. Park also confirmed with the department manager that no performance concerns had been raised about any team member named "Dana."

Resolution

The department manager was asked to reinforce customer service expectations with the entire team at the next team meeting, without referencing the complaint or any specific individual. The EthicsLine will remain open should the tipster wish to provide additional details. No further action at this time.

ⓘ
Key takeaway: A Level 3 closure does not mean the complaint was ignored. It means the organization received the complaint, assessed it through the established process, and made a documented, defensible decision about how to address it without a formal investigation.

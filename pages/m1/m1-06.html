Module 1: The Investigative Framework

Introducing the Henderson File

Your anchor case study for the entire course

Throughout this course, you will follow a single investigation from intake through closure. This fictional case—the Henderson File—is designed to give you hands-on practice applying each concept and procedure you learn. At the end of each module, you will return to the Henderson File to apply what you have covered.

Here is the initial complaint that starts the investigation:

The Henderson File — Initial Complaint

An anonymous complaint has been submitted to Maple Leaf Foods' EthicsLine hotline. The caller, who identified themselves only as "a concerned team member," provided the following information:

EthicsLine Report — Intake Summary

Date Received: Monday, October 14

Reporting Channel: EthicsLine Hotline (anonymous call)

Facility: Henderson Road Processing Plant

Subject of Complaint: Marcus Chen, Plant Supervisor

The complainant alleges that Marcus Chen, a plant supervisor at the Henderson Road facility, has been falsifying production quality inspection logs for the past three months. According to the caller, Chen has been instructing line workers to skip certain inspection checkpoints during high-volume production runs, potentially compromising food safety standards.

The caller stated: "I've seen him do it multiple times. When we're behind on production targets, he tells us to just check the boxes on the inspection forms without actually doing the checks. I'm worried about what's getting through."

The caller did not provide their name or position but indicated they work on the production line at the Henderson Road plant.

Pause and Reflect

Before moving on, take a moment to think about what you have learned in this module and how it applies to this complaint. Consider the following questions:

What function(s) might be involved?

Think about the seven functions covered by the SOP. Which function or functions would likely have a role in investigating this complaint? Could this be a cross-functional matter?

What investigation level might this be?

Based on what you know about the three investigation levels, how would you initially classify this matter? Consider the potential severity, complexity, and risk involved. What factors support your classification?

What guiding principles apply?

All five guiding principles apply to every investigation, but think about which ones are particularly relevant given the specific circumstances of this complaint. For example, how does confidentiality apply when the reporter is anonymous? How does fairness apply to Marcus Chen at this stage?

ⓘ
No right or wrong answers yet. The purpose of these questions is to get you thinking about how the framework applies to a real scenario. You will revisit the Henderson File at the start of Module 2, where you will work through the intake and triage process in detail.

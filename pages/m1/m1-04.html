Module 1: The Investigative Framework

Three Investigation Levels

How matters are classified based on risk and complexity

Not every report or complaint warrants the same level of investigative effort. The SOP defines three investigation levels to ensure that resources are allocated appropriately and that the response is proportionate to the nature and severity of the matter.

During the Triage stage (Step 2 of the 7-step process), the Investigation Lead or Coordinator classifies the matter into one of the following levels:

Level Description Examples
Level 1
Significant		 Complex, high-risk matters requiring a formal investigation team, legal involvement, and detailed reporting. These investigations typically involve senior personnel, significant financial exposure, potential regulatory implications, or serious harm to individuals or the organization.

Characteristics:
  • Formal investigation team assembled
  • Legal Counsel consulted or directing
  • Detailed investigation plan required
  • Comprehensive written report
  • Typically completed within 30–60 days
  • Serious harassment or discrimination allegations
  • Fraud, theft, or financial misconduct
  • Major food safety incidents with potential public health impact
  • Workplace violence or threats
  • Significant regulatory violations
  • Matters involving senior leadership
  • Data breaches affecting sensitive information
Level 2
Minor		 Lower-risk matters that can be handled by a single investigator using standard procedures. These investigations are less complex but still require a structured approach to fact-finding and documentation.

Characteristics:
  • Single investigator assigned
  • Legal Counsel consulted as needed
  • Standard investigation plan
  • Summary written report
  • Typically completed within 14–30 days
  • Policy violations (attendance, conduct, use of company resources)
  • Minor interpersonal conflicts or conduct issues
  • Routine safety incidents without serious injury
  • Low-level compliance concerns
  • Non-critical process deviations
Level 3
No Formal Investigation		 A preliminary review determines that no formal investigation is needed. The matter is documented, and the file is closed. This level is used when the initial assessment reveals insufficient basis for investigation or when the matter can be resolved through other means.

Characteristics:
  • Preliminary review only
  • No formal investigation plan
  • Brief documentation of rationale for closure
  • Matter documented and archived
  • Typically resolved within 5–10 days
  • Anonymous tips lacking sufficient detail to pursue
  • Matters already resolved through other processes (e.g., mediation, performance management)
  • Issues that fall outside the scope of the SOP
  • Duplicate reports of an already-investigated matter
  • Matters referred to external authorities
ⓘ
Level can change: An investigation that begins as Level 2 may be escalated to Level 1 if new information reveals greater complexity or risk. Similarly, a matter initially classified as Level 1 may be reclassified downward if the preliminary findings indicate lower risk than originally assessed. Any reclassification must be documented.
⚠
Even Level 3 matters must be documented. Even when no formal investigation is conducted, the intake, preliminary review, and rationale for closure must be recorded and archived. This documentation protects the organization if the matter resurfaces or is challenged later.

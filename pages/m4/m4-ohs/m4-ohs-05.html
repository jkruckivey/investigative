Evidence Summary

CCTV Footage

The footage confirms that Anika Patel entered the forklift aisle at a location where the warning signage was completely obscured by stacked pallets. From her approach direction, no visible warnings indicated she was entering a forklift-only zone. James Rivera was operating the forklift at normal speed and reacted quickly to avoid the collision.

Physical Inspection

A walkthrough of the warehouse revealed three additional locations where safety signage was partially or fully obscured by stored materials. Floor markings delineating forklift zones were faded in several areas. There are no physical barriers separating the forklift aisle from pedestrian areas at any entry point—only painted lines and signage. The designated pedestrian route requires a 3-minute detour through a congested staging area.

Interview Findings

Seven of ten warehouse workers interviewed confirmed regularly using the forklift aisle as a shortcut. All cited the impractical pedestrian route as the reason. Two workers reported previous near-misses that were not formally reported. The forklift operator, James Rivera, stated the pedestrian appeared suddenly in his path and confirmed he was traveling at normal operational speed. The warehouse safety coordinator acknowledged that signage audits had not been conducted in over six months.

Documentary Evidence

Training records confirm both workers completed their required safety orientations. The warehouse traffic management plan was last updated 18 months ago and does not reflect current warehouse layout changes. Prior near-miss reports for the facility show no reports involving forklift-pedestrian interactions, despite interview evidence that unreported near-misses had occurred.

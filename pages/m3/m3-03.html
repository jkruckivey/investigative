Evidence Collection

Gathering, preserving, and documenting evidence with integrity

Four Types of Evidence

Investigations rely on evidence to establish facts and support conclusions. Evidence in workplace investigations falls into four categories. Click each type below to learn about its characteristics and collection considerations.

Chain of Custody Requirements

Chain of custody refers to the documented trail that records the collection, transfer, handling, and storage of evidence throughout the investigation. Maintaining an unbroken chain of custody is essential to demonstrating that evidence has not been tampered with, altered, or compromised.

For each piece of evidence collected, the investigator must document:

Element Description
What was collected A clear description of the item or document, including any identifying numbers or labels
When it was collected The date and time the evidence was obtained
From whom / where The source or custodian of the evidence and the location from which it was obtained
By whom The name of the person who collected or received the evidence
How it was stored The storage method and location used to secure the evidence
Any transfers A log of every handoff, including dates, times, and the names of individuals involved in the transfer
⚠
Preservation Obligations: Once an investigation is initiated, the organization has an obligation to preserve all evidence that may be relevant to the matter. This includes issuing preservation notices (sometimes called litigation holds) to custodians of potentially relevant records. Failure to preserve evidence can result in adverse inferences in legal proceedings, regulatory sanctions, and damage to the credibility of the investigation. Never delete, alter, or allow the routine destruction of records that may be relevant to an active investigation.
ⓘ
Coordinating with IT and Records Management: Digital evidence and electronically stored documents are often subject to automated retention and deletion policies. As soon as an investigation is initiated, coordinate with Information Security and Records Management to ensure that relevant data is excluded from routine purges and preserved for the duration of the investigation.
